## Part 1
### 1.1 Audio Recording
**Common mistakes**
1. Overestimating _physical_ range of mic
2. Underestimating _audible_ range of mic
3. Clipping (distorting) audio beyond repair
[Understanding mic limitations: Entourage (coarse language)](https://youtu.be/jbHHF94ZrLI)

| Concept | Description | Representation | Recommendations |
|:-------:|:-----------:|:--------------:|:---------------:|
| **Reverb** | Reflection of sound waves back to origin/input; more instant and indistinct | N/A | 1. Distance from hard, flat surfaces <br>2. Cover hard, flat surfaces with soft, irregular ones (towels, blankets, etc) |
| **Echo** | Reflection of sound waves back to origin/input; more delayed and distict | N/A | 1. Avoid very large spaces |
| **Volume** | Measurement of loudness | Decibels (dB) | 1. Use optimal mic placement (1-2 hand spaces from mouth) <br>2. Adjust input volume for general peaks of -12 to -10dB |
| **Gain** | Measurement of change to volume | Decibels (dB) | 1. Use optimal mic placement (1-2 hand spaces from mouth) <br>2. Adjust input gain for volume peaks of -12 to -10dB |
| **Channel** | Input/output path for audio stream (lane in road) | <ul><li>Mono: One channel <li>Stereo: Two channels <li>Surround: Four or more channels</ul> | 1. Mono for each person, or <br>2. Stereo for each person |

#### General Tips
1. Look up, look down, look all around
    - Hard, flat surfaces (walls, floors, ceilings, furniture) affect recording through reverb
2. Put mic close, though not too close
    - People: 1-2 hand spaces from mouth
3. Adjust input settings and test
    - People: -12 to -10 dB volume peaks
    - Use standby/preview mode of device or make test recording
4. Recording some silence (3-5 sec) before and after
5. If possible, have secondary recording device
    - People: Clap at beginning to make editing (synching) easier
6. Scripts: Use digital notes instead of paper notes
7. Interviews: Ask interviewee to incorporate questions into answers
8. Dialogue: Mistakes--stop, pause (2-3 sec), and re-start sentences as needed

### 1.2. Audio Editing (Audacity)
1. Consider semi-scripted format
2. Adjust volume of recorded files as needed
    - Primary audio (dialogue): -12 to -6 dB for general volume peaks
    - Secondary audio (music, effects): -24 to -18 dB for general volume peaks
3. Incorporate sounds for flourish
    - [Using sounds for effect: Killers of the Flower Moon (2023) \[Spoilers\]](https://youtu.be/dqj7pjsdAfs?si=683SqWT1MGXULQpT) (YouTube video, 3 min 7 sec)
4. Leave pauses between utterances
